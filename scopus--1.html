Scopus Indexed Journals

What is scopus indexed journal?

Scopus is a bibliographic database containing abstracts and citations for academic articles. Scopus is owned by Elsevier and is available online by subscription. Searches in Scopus also incorporate searches of patent databases. Scopus covers – nearly 22,000 titles from over 5000 publishers of which 20,000 are peer –reviewed journals in all disciplines(Updated 2023).

Why Journal Indexing Scopus considered?


Over the year Scopus became famous for its quality and standard Indexed Journals. Indexation of a journal is considered a reflection of its quality. Scopus Indexed journals are considered to be of higher scientific quality as compared to other popular indexation services like, PubMed, EMBASE, EBSCO, MedLine, SCIRUS etc. Publishing in a Scopus indexed journal is an indication and recognition of the high quality of your work. It also increases the likelihood of getting cited and hence your citation score will go up. That improves the visibility of the university. Scopus follows very strict guidelines in terms of quality of journals and proceedings. Publishing in a non-indexed journal may not give you the recognition you deserve.

Common Reasons Why Research Papers Are Rejected in journal with Scopus index


Getting your research paper rejected can be disheartening, especially when targeting journal with Scopus Index. After investing years in research and spending months crafting and formatting your paper, it can be disheartening to witness your hard work seemingly wasted. If you often experience rejection or are looking for ways to avoid it, there's no reason to worry. In this article, we have compiled a list of common reasons why articles are declined by esteemed journal with Scopus Index. These reasons can be categorized into two groups: editorial and technical. By ensuring you avoid these mistakes when submitting your work to a journal, you can significantly enhance your chances of acceptance.

 Editorial Reasons for Research Paper Rejection

Lack of Alignment with the Journal's Aim and Scope

When submitting to a journal with Scopus Index, it is essential to ensure that your manuscript aligns with the specific mission and scope of the journal, as stated on its website. Neglecting this crucial aspect is a common oversight among new researchers who often submit their manuscripts without careful consideration. To improve your chances of being accepted, it is recommended that you conduct a comprehensive investigation into the objectives and focus of the journal prior to submitting your paper.

Incomprehensible Writing

Journals seek manuscripts that are written in a clear and easily understandable manner. Excessive use of jargon, poor diction, grammar errors, spelling mistakes, and missing essential text elements (such as figures, tables, author lists, and affiliations) are common reasons for paper rejection. If you encounter difficulties in writing in English, there are several tools available that can assist you in improving your research writing.

One of the main reasons for rejection is not following the writing guidelines established by the journal. Hence, it is of utmost importance to thoroughly examine the guidelines provided by the journal you intend to submit your work to.



 Technical Reasons for Research Paper Rejection

To increase the possibility of paper acceptance, it is essential to proactively identify and resolve any technical errors that may occur throughout the research journey. Unlike editorial reasons, these mistakes are typically related to the execution of the study and may require significant effort and time to rectify. In some cases, researchers may even need to start their work anew. To ensure a smooth submission process to a journal indexed in Scopus, it is advisable to proactively mitigate these technical errors. By staying vigilant and proactive, you can increase the likelihood of avoiding paper rejection altogether.

Research paper doesn’t add value to the journal: In certain cases, research papers may not be considered favorable by journal indexed in Scopus, particularly if the findings do not significantly contribute to the progress of their respective fields. If that is the situation, it would be more advisable for you to select a different journal indexed in Scopus.

Lack of supporting evidence: In certain instances, the information gathered during the research may be insufficient to support the conclusion put forth in the paper. If the sample size is small or if the control is not clearly defined, this situation can occur. If the data collected does not align with the hypothesis of a research paper, it is highly likely that the journal indexed in Scopus will reject the paper.

Wrong research methodology: The utilization of outdated research methodologies, when more advanced methods that yield more precise outcomes are available, may result in elimination. It is highly probable that the obtained results are unreliable, considering that there are more effective research methods indexed in Scopus.

Violation of research ethics:A strict code of rules governs the behavior in academia. In order to ensure ethical standards are met, it is necessary to obtain written consent from any participants involved in the research. Additionally, it is important to declare that the research has been conducted by a single individual and is free from plagiarism. Similar ethical guidelines should also be followed. Failure to do so by a researcher will result in not only the rejection of their paper but also the examination of it by a journal indexed in Scopus.

Unclear hypothesis: A hypothesis is a researcher's statement outlining their objectives and intentions. After conducting the research, the hypothesis is either confirmed or refuted. Research papers that lack a well-defined hypothesis or focus on a hypothesis that has been previously investigated and confirmed will not be considered by journal with Scopus index.


Poor analysis: When conducting research, the final step is typically to perform statistical analysis on the obtained results. On the other hand, it can be challenging to select the right statistical method for analyzing the findings of the research. The analysis conducted was incorrect and an inappropriate technique was utilized, which raises doubts about the validity of the results. As a result, the journal with Scopus index rejected the findings.

Inconclusive result: If a journal indexed in Scopus meets an inconclusive result or fails to provide an answer to the hypothesis, it is seen as a significant concern. The primary reason for rejecting a paper listed in Scopus is this particular aspect, which stands out among others.
